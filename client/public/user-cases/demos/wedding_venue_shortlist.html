Shortlist

Four Venues, Each with a Different Kind of Magic

Each option below reflects your original design direction while showing a different balance of architecture, atmosphere, guest experience, and logistics.

Think of this as a first-round shortlist to help you notice which setting feels most like your wedding.

Legion of Honor

$12K-$14K
A Beautiful Overall Match
Why it may feel right

Strongly matches your visual-first, museum-style preference. The venue gives the wedding a refined San Francisco identity without needing a very large guest count.

Something to keep in mind

Because the space is visually iconic, floral and rental choices may become more expensive if you want a full statement look.

A note from Lina

This is a beautiful place to start if you want to see your most museum-forward option first and decide how formal or dramatic you want the celebration to feel.

Museum setting Photogenic architecture Elevated guest arrival Possible overtime cost

Asian Art Museum

$20K-$22K
A Refined Cultural Alternative
Why it may feel right

Offers an art-forward setting with strong architectural character. It may feel especially meaningful if you want a refined city wedding with more cultural texture.

Something to keep in mind

May require careful design direction to avoid feeling too formal or institutional.

A note from Lina

This is a strong alternative if you love museum spaces but want something that feels a little more urban, layered, and indoors-focused.

Indoor setting Cultural atmosphere Architectural backdrop Design-dependent warmth

Flood Mansion

$15K-$19K
A Warm and Easy Guest Experience
Why it may feel right

Works well if you want an elevated wedding that still feels warm, comfortable, and easy for guests to navigate.

Something to keep in mind

Less museum-iconic than Legion of Honor, but potentially easier for dinner, speeches, and family moments.

A note from Lina

If comfort, flow, and an easy experience for family and guests start to matter more than pure editorial drama, this one becomes especially compelling.

Classic SF venue Strong indoor flow Family-friendly reception environment Polished but less dramatic

Conservatory of Flowers

$15K-$17K
A Romantic Garden Direction
Why it may feel right

Gives you a lush, romantic, San Francisco-specific setting. Natural surroundings may reduce the need for heavy floral installations.

Something to keep in mind

Outdoor logistics, weather backup, transportation, and setup timing may be more complex.

A note from Lina

If you respond most emotionally to soft garden romance rather than museum formality, this may be the option that feels most naturally you.

Natural beauty Garden setting Strong photo potential Weather/logistics sensitivity
